Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis

The Kaplan-Meier estimator is the most common way to estimate survival statistics. It allows researchers to visualize the probability of survival over time, even when some patients drop out of the study (censored data).

The Survival Curve

A Kaplan-Meier plot typically shows:

  • X-axis: Time (days, months, years) from the start of the study.
  • Y-axis: Cumulative Survival Probability (0 to 1, or 0% to 100%).
  • Steps: The curve drops each time an event (e.g., death) occurs.
  • Tick marks: Indicate censored patients (lost to follow-up) who have not experienced the event yet.
