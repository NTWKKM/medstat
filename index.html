MedStat: Free Alternative to SPSS, Stata, and GraphPad for Medical Research

MedStat is a comprehensive, free-to-use medical statistics platform designed specifically for researchers, clinicians, biostatisticians, and healthcare professionals. Our tool provides advanced analytical capabilities without the need for expensive software licenses or installation.

Troubleshooting Common Statistical Problems

Researchers often encounter errors that standard software handles poorly. MedStat addresses specific challenges:

  • Fix "Complete Separation" Errors: Standard logistic regression often fails with "complete separation" or infinite standard errors. MedStat uses Firth Logistic Regression to solve this and provide accurate estimates.
  • Analyze Small Sample Sizes: Avoid bias in small datasets (n < 50) using penalized likelihood methods designed for rare events and medical studies.
  • Observational Data Bias: Reduce confounding variables in non-randomized studies using Propensity Score Matching (PSM).

Publication-Ready Outputs

Generate results ready for academic journals:

  • Create Forest Plots Online: Automatically generate and customize high-resolution forest plots for odds ratios and hazard ratios.
  • APA Style Tables: Get statistical tables formatted for immediate inclusion in manuscripts.
  • Calculators: Instant p-value calculation and confidence interval generation.

Support for Excel and CSV

No need for complex file conversion. MedStat supports:

  • Analyze Excel Data Online: Upload .xlsx or .xls files directly.
  • CSV Statistics Tool: Native support for Comma Separated Values files.

Advanced Statistical Methods Available:

  • Firth Logistic Regression: A robust method to handle small sample sizes and complete separation in binary classification problems. Particularly valuable for clinical trials and medical research where sample sizes may be limited.
  • Propensity Score Matching (PSM): Reduce selection bias in observational studies by matching treated and control subjects. Essential for improving causal inference in non-randomized medical research.
  • Survival Analysis: Professional Kaplan-Meier survival curves and Cox Proportional Hazards Regression for time-to-event data analysis. Critical for medical research involving patient outcomes and mortality studies.
  • Biostatistics Dashboard: User-friendly interface for complex medical data analysis with intuitive visualization and reporting capabilities.
  • Cox Regression: Advanced survival analysis modeling for multivariate survival data in clinical studies.
